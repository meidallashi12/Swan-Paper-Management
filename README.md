Swan Paper Management
A comprehensive management analysis framework covering the end-to-end operations of a hygenic paper fabric manufacturing company, from raw material procurement and tissue machine performance to quality assurance, financial control, and workforce management.

 -- Overview
Swan Paper Management is built around the specific demands of continuous-process, capital-intensive manufacturing environments. Hygenic paper production, covering toilet tissue, kitchen rolls, facial tissues, and industrial wipes, requires precise coordination across procurement, production, quality, maintenance, logistics, and finance. A breakdown in any one of these functions ripples across the entire operation.
Swan Paper Management addresses this by providing a structured analytical framework for every layer of the business, giving operators, supervisors, and executives a clear model for how each function should be organized, measured, and managed.

 -- Analysis Areas
Production & Operations Management
Analysis of the full tissue manufacturing cycle, from stock preparation and tissue machine operation to converting, slitting, packaging, and finished goods transfer. The production management framework defines how performance should be measured, how shifts should be structured, and how the manufacturing process should be governed day-to-day.
Key areas covered:

OEE (Overall Equipment Effectiveness) as the primary production KPI, with Availability, Performance, and Quality components defined
Shift management structure using a 4-crew, 12-hour rotating pattern for 24/7 continuous operations
Broke and reject classification with root cause analysis methodology
Shift handover protocols and supervisor accountability frameworks
Parent reel and finished goods traceability from machine to pallet

 -- Supply Chain & Procurement
Analysis of end-to-end supply chain management covering the sourcing, purchasing, receiving, and consumption of all input categories, including virgin pulp, recycled deinked pulp (DIP), chemicals, packaging materials, and energy. The framework covers how procurement decisions should be structured, how suppliers should be managed, and how inventory should be controlled.
Key areas covered:

Supplier qualification process covering quality systems, financial stability, sustainability credentials, and logistics capability
Inventory policy with safety stock targets per material category (fiber: 15-20 production days; chemicals: 30 days)
ABC/XYZ material classification to prioritize procurement effort
Inbound logistics management including container shipment monitoring for imported pulp
Purchase price variance (PPV) analysis against standard cost

 -- Quality Control & Compliance
Analysis of in-line and finished goods quality management aligned with ISO 9001 and EN 12625 (tissue paper standard). The quality framework defines how product attributes should be monitored, how non-conformances should be handled, and how compliance should be maintained across the operation.
Key areas covered:

SPC (Statistical Process Control) methodology for critical quality attributes: basis weight (gsm), tensile strength (MD/CD), elongation, caliper, brightness, and absorbency
Non-conformance (NCR) management with corrective action (CAPA) process definition
Finished goods release protocols with QA sign-off requirements before dispatch
Batch traceability methodology linking finished SKUs to raw material lots and production parameters
Certificate of Analysis (CoA) requirements for B2B and export customers

 -- Maintenance & Asset Management
Analysis of structured maintenance management for tissue machines, converting lines, utilities, and ancillary equipment, built around a Total Productive Maintenance (TPM) philosophy that distributes responsibility between maintenance technicians and machine operators.
Key areas covered:

Preventive maintenance scheduling based on calendar intervals, machine hours, or condition triggers
Work order management with labor time tracking and spare parts consumption logging
Critical spare parts inventory policy with minimum stock levels and lead time considerations
Breakdown classification framework (mechanical, electrical, process, operational) for downtime analysis
Predictive maintenance methodology covering bearing vibration, temperature, and lubrication monitoring
Annual shutdown planning for Yankee cylinder and press section overhauls

 -- Financial & Cost Management
Analysis of the cost management framework for tissue manufacturing, where raw material and energy costs represent 60-70% of the total cost of goods sold and marginal cost improvements translate directly to EBITDA.
Key areas covered:

Standard product costing per SKU with monthly variance analysis methodology (actual vs. standard)
Cost-per-tonne breakdown by fiber, chemicals, energy, packaging, and labor
Capex project evaluation framework with NPV/payback analysis criteria
Working capital management targets: DSO, DPO, inventory days, and Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC)
Management reporting cadence: daily production cost flash, weekly cost summary, monthly P&L

 -- Workforce & Shift Management
Analysis of workforce management for 24/7 rotating shift operations typical in continuous process manufacturing, covering scheduling, competency tracking, and health & safety compliance across all plant personnel.
Key areas covered:

4-crew rotating shift pattern structure with supervisor accountability at each level
Operator skills matrix and certification tracking with defined training pathways
Safety incident classification (near-miss, first aid, LTI) and investigation methodology
HSE compliance framework aligned with ISO 45001
Training records management and succession planning principles

 -- Sustainability & ESG
Analysis of environmental performance management integrated into core operational reporting, rather than treated as a separate compliance function. Water, energy, and carbon performance are tracked at the operational level and aggregated per tonne of production.
Key areas covered:

Specific energy consumption (kWh/t) and specific water consumption (m³/t) as standard operational KPIs
Carbon footprint methodology per product SKU using defined emission factors
FSC/PEFC fiber certification compliance requirements across the supply chain
ESG reporting framework aligned with GRI Standards
Recycled fiber content tracking and reporting for product labeling compliance


 -- Analysis Goals
Swan Paper Management is structured to deliver clarity across the following operational dimensions:

Uptime, reducing unplanned downtime through structured preventive and predictive maintenance management
Yield, improving specific fiber consumption and reducing reject rates through in-line quality monitoring and SPC
Cost Visibility, establishing daily cost-per-tonne visibility across all variable cost categories
Compliance, defining the management requirements for ISO 9001, ISO 14001, ISO 45001, and ISO 50001 certification
Organizational Clarity, establishing clear roles, reporting lines, and accountability at every level of the company
Scalability, providing a framework applicable to single-site operations as well as multi-plant, multi-country manufacturing groups
