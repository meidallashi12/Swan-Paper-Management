# Stakeholder Analysis — Tissue & Fabric Manufacturing

| Stakeholder | Stakeholder Role / Responsibility | Importance | Influence | Interests / Positive Impacts | Concerns |
|---|---|:---:|:---:|---|---|
| Board of Directors | Strategic oversight, capital allocation, and executive accountability for company performance | High | High | Strong ROI, brand growth, market share expansion, sustainable dividends | Cost overruns, reputational risk from quality failures, ESG compliance exposure |
| Chief Executive Officer (CEO) | Overall strategy, investor relations, and cross-functional alignment across production and sales | High | High | Market leadership, operational excellence, stakeholder trust | Supply chain disruptions, competitive pricing pressure, talent retention |
| Chief Operating Officer (COO) | Day-to-day manufacturing, quality control, and logistics operations | High | High | Streamlined production cycles, reduced waste, improved throughput | Machine downtime, workforce shortages, raw material variability |
| Chief Financial Officer (CFO) | Budgeting, cost accounting, treasury, and financial reporting | High | High | Cost reduction, accurate forecasting, healthy cash flow | Commodity price volatility, capex overruns, currency fluctuations |
| Chief Supply Chain Officer (CSCO) | End-to-end supply chain, vendor relationships, and material flow from raw fiber to finished goods | High | High | Reliable sourcing, lower lead times, diversified supplier base | Raw material shortages, single-source dependency, logistics delays |
| Plant Manager | On-site production performance, safety compliance, and team management | High | Medium | Efficient shift scheduling, reduced defect rates, safe working environment | Equipment failures, labor disputes, production target pressure |
| Procurement Manager | Supplier management, purchasing workflows, and inventory control for fibers and chemicals | High | Medium | Competitive pricing, sustainable sourcing, reduced inventory holding costs | Supplier quality inconsistency, lead time variability, price inflation |
| QA Manager | Quality standards, compliance testing, and non-conformance management for fabric products | High | Medium | Fewer product recalls, clear quality benchmarks, regulatory approval | Balancing speed-to-market with rigorous testing, lab resource limitations |
| Chief Engineer | Asset maintenance, capex projects, and energy management across weaving and finishing lines | High | Medium | Modern equipment, lower energy costs, predictive maintenance adoption | Aging machinery, high capex requirements, integration of new technology |
| HR & HSE Manager | Workforce planning, training, safety compliance, and labor relations in factory environment | High | Medium | Skilled workforce, low accident rates, positive workplace culture | High employee turnover, chemical exposure risks, shift work fatigue |
| Sales & Marketing Director | Customer relationships, pricing strategy, and channel management for B2B and retail clients | High | Medium | Growing order volumes, strong brand positioning, new market penetration | Demand forecasting accuracy, competitor pricing, customer churn |
| Finance Controller | Product costing, variance analysis, and management reporting for manufacturing operations | Medium | Medium | Accurate cost tracking, timely reporting, margin visibility per product line | Data quality from production systems, complexity of cost allocations |
| Retail / B2B Customers | End buyers of tissue and fabric products; drive demand signals and product specifications | High | High | Consistent product quality, competitive pricing, reliable delivery schedules | Supply shortages, quality variations, limited product customization options |
| Environmental / Regulatory Bodies | Set environmental standards for effluent discharge, chemical usage, and emissions in textile production | High | High | Industry compliance, reduction in chemical waste, adoption of cleaner production methods | Non-compliance penalties, water treatment requirements, carbon reporting obligations |
| Raw Material Suppliers | Provide cotton, synthetic fibers, dyes, and chemicals essential to the manufacturing process | High | Medium | Long-term contracts, volume growth, fair payment terms | Payment delays, price renegotiations, loss of key account |
| Factory Workers / Labor Unions | Operate looms, finishing lines, and packaging equipment; represent workforce interests | High | Medium | Fair wages, safe working conditions, job security, skills development | Automation replacing roles, shift patterns, chemical exposure, insufficient benefits |
| System Investors / Funders | Provide capital for plant expansion, equipment upgrades, and working capital | High | High | Return on investment, strong EBITDA margins, scalable business model | Cost overruns, timeline delays, commodity price risk, ESG compliance |
| Local Community | Indirect stakeholders affected by factory operations, employment opportunities, and environmental impact | Medium | Low | Local employment, community investment, reduced pollution from modern facilities | Noise, water pollution, chemical odors, disruption during expansion projects |
